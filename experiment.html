Quantum Immortality

Imagine being Schr√∂dinger's cat - trapped in a room but with a qubit (think of it as a binary bit) that determines your life and death.

Whenever you press the button, it has a 50/50 chance of being in either states. Long story short, under the many-worlds interpretation, a superposition of the live you necessarily exists (and so does the you that dies). Assuming that the dead doesn't have any consciousness, only the live one is capable of having any sort of conscious experience. As you press the button nth times, there is a version of you that survives all nth iterations of the experiment even as n tends to infinity. Hence, the notion of quantum immortality.

Technically, you can say that every time a 50/50 choice is made, a superposition is created which then splits the timeline into 2. The coin on the left has a 50/50 chance to be either red or grey.So whenever you make a decision to press it, you will be splitting the timeline into 2.
